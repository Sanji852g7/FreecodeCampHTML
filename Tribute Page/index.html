STEVE IRWIN

Stephen Robert Irwin, known as "The Crocodile Hunter", was an Australian zookeeper, conservationist, television personality, wildlife educator, and environmentalist

Achievements and accomplishments

  • February 22nd, 1962:
    Stephen Robert Irwin was born in Upper Ferntree Gully, Victoria, Australia.
  • June 3rd, 1970:
    Steve Irwin's family opened a reptile park in Queensland and called it the "Beerwah Reptile Park and Fauna Park. "
  • 1982:
    The park was re-named the "Beerwah Reptile Park" to "Queensland Reptile".
  • 1990:
    Steve Irwin discovered a rare species of freshwater turtle on the Burdekin River,Queensland.The turtle was then named after him;Elseya irwini
  • 1991:
    Steve Irwin met Terri Rains and married a year later in 1992.
  • 1996:
    Instead of a honeymoon, the newlyweds embarked on a expedition filming for nature documentary that would later turn into the hit TV series,"The Crocodile Hunter".The Crocodile Hunter became successful in the United States, the UK, and over 130 other countries, reaching 500 million people.
  • July 25th, 1998:
    Bindi Sue Irwin was born
  • 2000:
    Steve Irwin received a sunshine coast Tourism memorial award in 2000 for most outstanding contribution to tourism. He also received a Honorary Senior Fellow by the University of the Sunshine Coast.
  • 2001:
    Steve Irwin received a Centenary Medal for his contribution to global conservation and tourism.
  • 2002:
    The Crocodile Hunter Movie was released.In the same year he received a Queensland Museum Medalist for his work in conservation and winner of Brisbane's Best Export Award
  • 2003:
    Steve Irwin received Queenslander of the year.
  • December 1st, 2003:
    Robert Clarence Irwin was born
  • 2004:
    Nominated for Australian of the year and was recognized as Tourism Export of the Year
  • September 4th, 2006:
    Steve Irwin Died
  • 2007:
    ~Steve Irwin was named the favourite male personality on pay TV at the ASTRA Awards
    ~Inducted into the Logies Hall of Fame
    ~The Sea Shepherd Conservation Society rename its ship 'Steve Irwin' in honour of Steve's conservation efforts.
    ~The Rwandan Government named a Baby Gorilla after Steve as tribute to his work in wildlife conservation.
    ~The Crocodile Rehabilitation and Research Centre in Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary was named by the Kerala government after Steve.
    ~Awarded the Adjunct Professorship posthumously by the University of Queensland's School of Integrative Biology
  • 2009:
    ~Steve Irwin received a khai- coloured treenail named Crikey steveirwini in memory of Steve.
    ~Queensland honoured Steve's contributions, naming him as one of the twelve 'greats of Queensland business' to be inducted into the Queensland Business Leaders Hall of Fame.
    ~Commemorated on the Royal Australian Mint's Inspirational Australian series, appearing as an uncirculated $1 coin.
